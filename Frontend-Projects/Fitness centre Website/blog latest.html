First Day at Gym

7 JULY 2022

This is my first blog and in this blog, I’ll share a few tips which will profit you prior to joining a gym, and furthermore, I’ll share my experience on my first day at the gym. I always wanted to join a gym because it gives you access to a wider variety of exercise equipment, so it’s easier for you to get a complete, full-body workout. Moreover, you’re less likely to get bored. Gyms are also social in nature because you get to work out with other people — they are places to see and be seen.
I still remember my first day at the gym and I really can’t express how excited I was. You remember when our school used to take us on one-day trips and how much excited we used to be to wake up go get ready, that was my excitement level. I woke up at sharp 5:00 am, brushed my teeth, had a glass of warm water, and had some dry fruits. I reached the gym and I was amazed by its ambiance. I quickly wore my gym shoes and met my trainer. The trainer gave me a detailed explanation of the different gym exercises, diet and also asked me a few questions. My gym started with some warmup exercises, and later, I did a cardio workout as it was my cardio day. Finally, I ended up my first gym workout with some stretching exercises which are a must guys please remember, never skip stretching, or probably you may wind up with torment in your body. The trainer was so kind and guided me very well throughout my first day. After my first workout, I was extremely hungry and I had a slice of brown bread with peanut butter on top, two boiled eggs, one banana, and a glass of milk. That was my awesome experience on my first day at the gym.

I lost 20 kgs with PCOS by going dairy and gluten-free just because of trainer of Fat to Fit.

23 APRIL 2022

I had been overweight for a couple of years and put on around 20 kilos after finishing college in 2017, all due to poor lifestyle and stress. The pattern continued in 2019, and I was diagnosed with PCOS and Hashimotos Thyroiditis (autoimmune condition). These conditions are faily common now. But it doesn't mean it has to be normal. When I was handed over my diagnosis- along with medicines- it really did shook me. This was truly the turning point for me. So, I decided there on to bring in changes- no matter at what cost. I began to exercise twice a day and eat healthy, but still struggled for a while with losing weight. This made me dig deep in to my own condition and with the help of Ayurveda literature and functional medicine literature – I found some of the answers. And finally with the right knowledge and application I managed to drop 20 kilos in six months. Although it took me some time to figure out the puzzle by addressing root cause issues - it worked ! I was finally there !
I primarily followed a gluten-free and dairy-free diet. Going gluten-free may not be required for everyone but because I had a leaky gut as well I did this. But going dairy-free is something I highly recommend for everyone. - I began my day with a green vegetable smoothie topped with chia seeds. This was to control insulin levels in the body, as PCOS is a precursor for diabetes. - My lunch would be a bajra bhakri or brown rice with a bowl of vegetables or salad + daal. - Evening snack is ideally the most important – I’d have a bowl of sprouts chaat, makhana bhel or fruits topped with mixed nuts and seeds. - I would eat my dinner by 7:30 pm and it would be grilled fish or chicken with a bowl of soup or salad. On days I was hungrier I’d eat my meal with some rice.
