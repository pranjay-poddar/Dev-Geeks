Bhagavad Gita Excerpt 1:
"You have the right to perform your prescribed duties, but you are not entitled to the fruits of your actions. Never consider yourself to be the cause of the results of your activities, and never be attached to not doing your duty."

Bhagavad Gita Excerpt 2:
"Perform your duties efficiently, O Arjuna, without attachment to success or failure. Such equanimity is called Yoga."

Bhagavad Gita Excerpt 3:
"When meditation is mastered, the mind is unwavering like the flame of a lamp in a windless place."

Bhagavad Gita Excerpt 4:
"The wise, who are free from the delusion of egoism, who have conquered the attachment of this world, and who are constantly engaged in selfless service for the welfare of all, they reach the highest state of bliss."

Bhagavad Gita Excerpt 5:
"Renounce attachment to the fruits of actions, and perform your duties with an even mind. Those who work in this manner are unaffected by sinful reactions."

Bhagavad Gita Excerpt 6:
"One who remains unattached under all circumstances, and who is neither elated by success nor depressed by failure, is truly wise."

Bhagavad Gita Excerpt 7:
"See the Divine in all beings, and all beings in the Divine. Then, the delusion of ego will vanish, and the innate unity of all existence will be realized."
